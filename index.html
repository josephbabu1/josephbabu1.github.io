I'm a second-year Electronics and Communication Engineering student at Government Engineering College, Thrissur. My work centers on embedded systems, digital signal processing, and FPGA-based hardware design — with a particular interest in building physical-layer security directly into communication hardware rather than treating it as a software afterthought.

Current projects span CAN bus telemetry for an active electric go-kart racing team and a chaos-driven convolutional codec for lightweight physical-layer security.

Current Research Focus

Adaptive FEC for chiplet die-to-die interconnects

Designing a rate-adaptive forward error correction engine for UCIe-style D2D links in chiplet-based NoCs. The codec monitors real-time link SNR and dynamically switches between BCH code rates — trading bandwidth for error-correction strength only when channel conditions demand it, instead of paying a fixed FEC overhead at all times.

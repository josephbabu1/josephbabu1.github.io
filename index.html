ABOUT ME

I'm Joseph Babu, a 2nd year Electronics and Communication Engineering student at Government Engineering College, Thrissur. Passionate about bridging hardware and software through embedded systems, digital signal processing, and FPGA development.

My engineering journey focuses on creating innovative solutions in communication systems, from chaos-driven codecs to real-time vehicle monitoring systems. I enjoy tackling complex challenges in digital design and signal processing.

Chaos-Driven Time-Varying Codec

Hardware implementation of a chaos-driven time-varying convolutional encoder with constraint length K=7. Dynamic generator polynomial variation using synchronized chaotic sequence for lightweight physical layer security. Implemented in Verilog HDL for FPGA.

Digital Voltmeter (ADC0808 + 8051)

3-channel digital voltmeter using 8051 microcontroller and ADC0808. Converts analog voltage (0-5V) to digital display on 16×2 LCD. Features 19.5mV resolution with <2% error. Implemented in Proteus with embedded C in Keil μVision.
